What comes next?

Raphael | Wed 20 May 2020

In the Broadway smash hit musical, the evil King George asks in one of his Beatles-sounding songs of the same name a simple question of the, at that point in the story victorious American revolutionaries:

What comes next?

The American experiment having won, the British forces vanquished, they start to negotiate terms of surrender. In the midst of these victory celebrations, King George asks the necessary question of what will happen now. A monumental cut in history has happened, where there will now be a distinct before and after - which we can clearly see today in effect still. The question "What comes next?" is apt and worth asking.
But amidst the chaos of the last few months, with most of us still (hopefully) social distancing and/or quarantining at home or wherever, I am not sure that we could possibly formulate an answer, were King George to rise from his grave and ask us the same thing; let alone imagine asking the question ourselves right now.
Aside from various more or less fantastical visions of the future that either prophecy the return to communism or mankind'a return to nature, the fact is that no one knows what will happen after this is over. And whether this is a symptom of the lefts (?) lack of imaginative power or a manifestation of capitalist realism, it's hard to even imagine that this will be over and anything will change at all. On top of that, it feels hard to imagine not only what will come after this pandemic, but also what came before. What was dominating the news cycle before we knew the names of now multiple virologists.

Why is that?
